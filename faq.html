FAQ

Questions

Common questions about the collection, the science inspiration, the planned marketplace direction, and the larger Syndicate universe.

What blockchain is The Syndicate Exoplanet Archive planned for?

The collection is planned for Solana, with marketplace access through Magic Eden. Solana was chosen because it is widely used for NFT collections, has fast transactions, and fits the goal of making the archive easier for collectors to access. Final marketplace details will be confirmed before the official launch.

What is The Syndicate Exoplanet Archive?

The Syndicate Exoplanet Archive is a science-fiction NFT collection built around real exoplanet inspiration. Each planet begins with astronomy-based data and is transformed into a cinematic archive entry with visual traits, rarity tiers, moons, rings, system features, and lore connected to The Syndicate universe.

Is this project affiliated with NASA?

No. This is an independent art and storytelling project. The collection is inspired by public astronomy and exoplanet research, but it is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by NASA.

Are the planets based on real planets?

Yes. The collection is inspired by real exoplanet names, discovery data, and science categories. The final artwork is fictional and cinematic, but the foundation of the archive comes from real astronomical discovery.

What makes each NFT different?

Each archive entry has its own planet name, token number, rarity tier, visual class, archetype, moons, rings, star system traits, and archive identity. Some planets are simple core archive worlds, while others have rare traits such as ring systems, multi-star systems, stronger visual signatures, or story importance inside The Syndicate universe.

What do the rarity tiers mean?

Rarity tiers help organize the archive by visual impact, system traits, and story potential. Common planets are core archive worlds. Uncommon planets have stronger traits or more interesting science features. Rare and Epic planets may include ring systems, unusual world types, or stronger visual treatments. Legendary and Mythic entries are reserved for major discoveries, story artifacts, or one-of-one archive pieces.

Who created the collection?

The collection is created by Peter Jackman, also known as Orca. This project is part of a larger creator journey involving AI-assisted learning, Blender, Python, digital art, worldbuilding, and independent publishing. The goal is to build a real NFT collection while also developing the larger Syndicate universe behind it.

How does the NFT collection connect to the novel?

The NFT collection is the main launch focus. The novel expands the universe behind the archive, but the planets stand on their own as collectible digital worlds. Over time, the book, lore, characters, and archive entries will connect more deeply, turning the collection into the foundation for a larger science-fiction world.

The Syndicate Exoplanet Archive is an independent sci-fi art project inspired by public astronomy data. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by NASA.
